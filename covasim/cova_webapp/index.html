About COVASim

COVASim is an agent-based model that simulates the transmission of COVID-19 in a population. Each individual in the model may pass through the following stages of COVID-19 infection: susceptible, exposed, infectious, and recovered (SEIR). The epidemiological parameter values are taken from literature.

Viral transmission occurs on a fixed contact network with undirected edges. Each day, infectious individuals expose susceptible individuals in neighboring nodes to possible infection. The daily probability of infection, number of contacts, and mean duration of infectiousness roughly equates to R0 = 2.3 and a doubling time of 6.2 days. For more information, please visit the GitHub repository.

Note: This site is intended to provide information about COVID-19. The work presented here originates from IDM research unless otherwise stated and attributed. This is a rapidly changing situation, and we will update content as relevant information is received.
